Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is PT-141?

PT-141 (Bremelanotide) is a synthetic peptide that activates melanocortin receptors in the brain to enhance sexual desire and arousal. Unlike traditional ED medications that work on blood flow, PT-141 works through the central nervous system to address both desire and physical response.

How is PT-141 different from Viagra?

While Viagra and similar PDE5 inhibitors work by increasing blood flow to the genitals, PT-141 works in the brain by stimulating melanocortin receptors and increasing dopamine production. This means it addresses both sexual desire (libido) and physical arousal, making it effective for people who don't respond to traditional ED medications.

How is PT-141 administered?

PT-141 is typically administered via subcutaneous injection or nasal spray approximately 30-60 minutes before sexual activity. The injection is given in the abdomen or thigh. Your physician will provide detailed instructions and training.

How quickly does PT-141 work?

PT-141 typically takes effect within 30-60 minutes after administration. The effects can last several hours, and it should be used on an as-needed basis rather than daily.

What are the side effects?

The most common side effect is nausea, which occurs in approximately 40% of patients but is typically mild and temporary. Flushing is also common but usually brief and mild. These side effects tend to decrease with continued use.

Is PT-141 FDA approved?

PT-141 (branded as Vyleesi) was FDA approved in 2019 for treating hypoactive sexual desire disorder (HSDD) in women. For men, its use is currently off-label but supported by clinical research showing effectiveness for erectile dysfunction.

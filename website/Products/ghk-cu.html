Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is GHK-Cu?

GHK-Cu (glycyl-L-histidyl-L-lysine) is a naturally occurring peptide found in human plasma, saliva, and urine. It complexes with copper to accelerate wound healing, stimulate collagen production, and support skin regeneration.

Why do GHK-Cu levels decline with age?

Plasma levels drop from approximately 200 ng/ml at age 20 to around 80 ng/ml by age 60. This decline contributes to reduced healing capacity, decreased collagen production, and visible aging signs.

How long until I see results?

Topical applications may show initial improvements in skin texture and hydration within 2-4 weeks. More significant changes in collagen production and skin firmness become apparent after 8-12 weeks of consistent use.

Is GHK-Cu better than retinoids?

Studies show GHK-Cu increased collagen production in 70% of women, compared to 50% for vitamin C cream and 40% for retinoic acid. GHK-Cu also has a long history of safe use with minimal irritation.

What formulations are available?

GHK-Cu is available as topical creams/serums, subcutaneous injections, and in combination with other peptides. Your physician will recommend the best formulation based on your goals.

Is GHK-Cu safe?

GHK-Cu has a long history of safe use in wound healing and skin care. It's naturally occurring, nontoxic, and active at very low nanomolar concentrations. Side effects are minimal.
